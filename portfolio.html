Sprinter Web-GIS
During this project, I developed a web application to manage 360-degree panoramic images of the West Qurna 2 oilfield located in Basrah, Iraq.
As part of the project, I developed the system’ web-interface, prepared and published the available spatial data. Then I automatically captured about 10,000 panoramic images to cover the main roads of the oilfield using the panoramic camera mounted on the roof of the car. Developed web-GIS provided users with a convenient and fast web interface for viewing panoramic images. All panoramic images were automatically linked to each other and users were able to view and navigate between panoramic images in the same way as Google StreetView. Also, users were able to quickly search and view various geographic information, such as a field plan and individual objects, measurements on a map, and search by attributes.
For the development of the user interface system, I used HeronJS javascript framework (based on OpenLayers and ExtJS) and KRPano panoramic viewer.  GeoServer was used as a backend, and a PostgreSQL database was used to store spatial information. Python scripts were written for the service functions (recalculation of coordinates, bookmarks on the map and shortening of links). Panoramic image processing (linking panoramic images to each other, creating XML files with information about the relationships between images) was performed using the created Workflow in FME Desktop.

TAMARA Project
The Taimyr wild reindeer herd is the largest and the longest monitored reindeer herd in Eurasia. The Extreme North Agricultural Research Institute in Norilsk, Russia carried out monitoring since 1969. However, monitoring data accumulated in the form of paper hard copies and the documentation was kept in Russian. The reports were not digitized and processed using modern geospatial technologies. Also, the only limited amount of the data was published, so these works remained inaccessible to most scientists. The goal of TAMARA the project is to digitize the data contained in the reports, produce digital maps and derivative products, identify the herd migration patterns, disseminate the derived datasets and publish the analysis results. Now we nearly completed the digitization of the data contained in the reports and created digital derived products showing the spatial distribution of reindeer herds and their migration process. This poster presentation will present the initial results of the digitization and analysis of the distribution of herds based on aerial census reports.
GIS2 - Dasymetric mapping of Iowa City, IA population

A population usually estimated by substantial census tracts/blocks which covering significant areas. Census tracts are protecting privacy, but not giving a good spatial representation of population distribution. My aim for this project was to accurately depict population distribution for the study area using the dasymetric mapping, which would show actual population distribution on the buildings level. 
In my term project, I developed a workflow in Safe Software FME allowing quick creation of dasymetric maps using the 3D volumetric method of dasymetric mapping and tested the workflow in Iowa City area, IA. Maps, showing population density and population counts at a building level were produced.
GIS Programming - GeoTREE LiDAR Data downloader

The Iowa state like the most other US states has publically available LiDAR coverage. Access to LiDAR data files is provided via a web GIS, created at GeoTree Center, University of Northern Iowa.
I developed the ArcGIS toolbox for easily downloading LiDAR data from a GeoTree web server directly from ArcGIS without using a web interface.
Users can select areas of interest, download LIDAR tiles, bare earth XYZI files and derived products, including DEM, Hillshade and Contours datasets.
The tool eliminates manual work and minimizes chances of downloading of unnecessary tiles, as we are downloading tiles failing into current map extent or extent of the requested layer only, and the user does not need to manually select areas of interest anymore, which potentially improves user' productivity.

GPS - Evaluation of the applicability of the medium-wave infrared range for the estimation of heat losses for City of Cedar Falls, IA
In my work, I decided to evaluate the capabilities of a professional GPS receiver to measure the volume of materials stored at the Cedar Falls Compost Facility site. In my project, I performed volume measurements of the stockpiles using Trimble survey-grade GPS in RTK mode.
As an outcome of the project, a plan was created depicting the placement of storage piles of materials and the assessment of volumes.
RS1 - Evaluation of supervised classification algorithms accuracy on Sentinel 2 data on Adair and Delaware Counties, Iowa
The goal of my course project was to assess the accuracy of the classification of Land Use Land Cover scenes with Sentinel 2 using various classification algorithms, such as the Artificial Neural Network, Maximum Likelihood, Support Vector Machine, and Random Forest algorithms. As part of the project, I performed a search and download of scenes from Sentinel 2 to the territory of interest, automatic geometric correction by linking to existing NAIP images, training classification algorithms, classification and assessment of accuracy. The work was using ENVI 5.5 software.
The best-performing methods in terms of overall accuracy for supervised of Land use/Land cover classification of Sentinel-2 imagery for rural US landscapes were Artificial Neural Network, Maximum Likelihood, Support Vector Machine, and Random Forest algorithms. Regarding processing time, the fastest methods were Maximum Likelihood and Random forest algorithms, and the slowest - Support Vector machine.
When the training data derived from the geometrically corrected ground truth image, the classification algorithms demonstrating steady but negligible accuracy increase around 1%. It could indicate that additional geometric correction of L1C Sentinel-2 products in most cases doesn’t worth the efforts.
Panoramic tours for UKAN Project, Uzbekistan
During this project, I created panoramic tours of the construction of the gas processing plant at the Kandym gas field located near Bukhara, Uzbekistan. Locations of the panoramic images were placed onto a map, where the user can select places of interest, and navigate between the images.
After data collection, to present the results to the end-users, portable desktop applications were created using the NW.js framework and KRPano panoramic viewer.
